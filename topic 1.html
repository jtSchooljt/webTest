Legislative

The Legislative branch is made of the house of representives and the senate. They together form the United States Congress.

Congress is the only part of the government that can make new laws or pass existing laws.

Although the president can veto bills, congress can overide a veto by a two-thirds vote.

The house of representitives is made of 435 elected members.

The senate has 100 senators, 2 for each state.
